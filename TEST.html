What is an Ionic Bond?

Ionic bonds are bonds that consist of a non-metal and a metal. the main charactics of ionic bonds is that it transfers valence electrons from the metal atom to the non-metal atom. Because of this both atoms become ions of their respective element thus giving the bond type its name. In the chemical formula the metal ion comes first then the non-metal ion.

What properties do Ionic Bonds have?

One property is that Ionic compounds, which are made up of Ionic bonds, have high melting and boiling points.

Aswell due to Ionic compound's strength they require a lot of energy to break up.

Ionic compounds also form crystals.

Ionic compounds are brittle thus making them easily break into small pieces.

Ionic compounds tend to dissolve into water and not in solvents such as oil, petrol and kerosene.

Although ionic compounds do not conduct electricity well in the solid state they do conduct electricity well in their molten state.

Examples of Ionic Bonds

Sodium Chloride or NaCl

Lithium Fluoride or LiF

Calcium chloride or Ca2Cl

Sodium Bromide or NaBr
